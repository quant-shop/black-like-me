BLACK LIKE ME

Despite growing attention to global exchanges in teacher training, few studies focus on teachers’ understanding of cultural differences, diaspora, and the role of HBCUs in advancing diaspora literacy. This project explores how international engagements shape educators’ awareness of the African diaspora and informs our practice.

Project Description

Diasporic awareness involves understanding shared histories and cultural practices (Boutte, Johnson, & Muki, 2019; Zeleza, 2009). This project draws on frameworks from Black and African diaspora studies, and emphasizes the role of HBCUs in diaspora literacy and educator preparation. Using a design thinking framework, this project centers collaboarative and international dialogues to expand conversations on how educators’ diasporic awareness shapes critical professional visions and collective practices. This project supports tools, modules, and open-source and collaborative spaces to advance diaspora literacy and global competence in teacher education.
